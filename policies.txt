PURPOSE
These HR policies define the basic rules, responsibilities, and expectations between the organization and its employees. They aim to create a fair, respectful, and productive workplace.

SCOPE
These policies apply to all employees of the organization, including permanent, temporary, contractual, and probationary employees, unless stated otherwise.

EMPLOYMENT PRACTICES
The organization follows fair and equal employment practices. Recruitment, promotion, training, and compensation decisions are based on merit, qualifications, performance, and business needs. Discrimination based on race, gender, religion, caste, age, disability, or any other protected characteristic is not permitted.

APPOINTMENT AND PROBATION
All new employees will receive an appointment letter outlining their role, compensation, and terms of employment. Employees may be placed on a probation period during which performance and conduct will be reviewed. Successful completion of probation will be confirmed in writing.

WORKING HOURS
Standard working hours will be defined by the organization and may vary by role or department. Employees are expected to adhere to assigned working hours and maintain punctuality. Any deviations must be approved by the reporting manager.

ATTENDANCE AND LEAVE
Employees must record attendance as per company procedure. Leave entitlements such as casual leave, sick leave, earned leave, and holidays will be communicated separately. Leave should be planned in advance and approved by the reporting manager, except in emergencies.

COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS
Employees will be compensated as per agreed terms in their appointment letter. Salary payments will be made on a monthly basis. Benefits, allowances, and bonuses, if any, will be governed by company policies and eligibility criteria.

PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT
Employee performance will be reviewed periodically through a formal appraisal process. Feedback will be provided to support growth and development. Performance outcomes may impact increments, promotions, or corrective actions.

CODE OF CONDUCT
Employees are expected to maintain professional behavior at all times. Honesty, integrity, respect, and accountability are core values. Any form of misconduct, including harassment, fraud, or misuse of company resources, may lead to disciplinary action.

WORKPLACE HARASSMENT
The organization is committed to providing a safe and respectful workplace. Harassment of any kind, including sexual harassment, is strictly prohibited. Complaints will be handled confidentially and fairly as per established procedures.

HEALTH AND SAFETY
Employees must follow all health and safety guidelines provided by the organization. Creating a safe work environment is a shared responsibility. Any unsafe conditions must be reported immediately.

CONFIDENTIALITY
Employees must protect confidential company information, including business data, client details, and intellectual property. Confidential information must not be disclosed during or after employment without proper authorization.

USE OF COMPANY ASSETS
Company assets such as laptops, systems, ID cards, and access credentials are to be used only for official purposes. Employees are responsible for the proper care and return of company property.

DISCIPLINARY ACTION
Violation of company policies may result in disciplinary action, ranging from verbal warnings to termination, depending on the severity of the offense. All actions will follow principles of fairness and due process.

SEPARATION FROM EMPLOYMENT
Employment may be terminated by the employee or the organization by providing notice as per the appointment letter. Employees must complete handover responsibilities and return company property before separation.

POLICY AMENDMENTS
The organization reserves the right to modify, update, or withdraw these policies at any time to meet business or legal requirements. Employees will be informed of any significant changes.
